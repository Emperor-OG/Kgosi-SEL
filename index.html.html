We are three education students united to reduce abuse and violence in schools. Our campaign is built on teamwork, research, and direct engagement with learners, teachers, and community members.

What we do

We address the urgent need to decrease abuse and violence in schools. Many learners do not know how to classify abuse or where to report. Our campaign provides education on different types of abuse, learners’ rights, and the importance of speaking up. We organize awareness sessions, create posters and educational materials, and build a website that students can easily access to help prevent abuse and violence. We also invite volunteer speakers (social workers, police officers) to present. Teachers help guide learners, and parents are informed and give their thoughts. Community members give talks on the subject. By Week 3 we launch the awareness campaign at school together with the website, and in Week 4 we collect feedback to improve.

Who we serve

We serve learners in primary schools who face abuse and violence. Teachers who need support in guiding learners through these situations are also key beneficiaries. Parents must be informed about the campaign and give out their thoughts, so we serve families as well. Finally, community members - social workers, police officers, local volunteers - are invited to give talks and become part of the solution. Our campaign aims to reach every person who can help a child feel safe. We believe that when learners know their rights and how to report, they can step above violence.
